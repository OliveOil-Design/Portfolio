I’m passionate about design that can positively impact sustainability and clean living in the future while providing great utility and a fun user experience. I am particularly interested in the intersection between design for the circular economy and exploring how we can redesign our most loved products to make sure that when they have reached their end of life, they can be returned safely to the earth to begin again as something new.

About Me.

Something Extra

I’ve always had a huge passion for sustainability, and I’ve tried to incorporate the ideals of leaving the future in a better state than what we inherited.
I’m always trying to learn more about how I can apply the best sustainability practices to the work I’m doing, because I really firmly believe that the future of design and making is rooted in the Green Transition.

With that in mind, when I saw the UK Design Council’s report on the lack of Green Design Skills among designers, I found it so interesting that I created a short guide that breaks down the report as well as gives a short overview of each of the Green Transition Skills we need to drive the green transition forward.

Follow the link below by clicking the image, and you’ll find yourself at the guide, please check it out and I hope you find it useful!

Master’s in Design For Emergent Futures

Recently I’ve spent time doing a Master’s in Design for Emergent Futures, where I was able to apply my passion for sustainable design with a new lens of looking to the preferable futures we want to see and working backwards from there to create the necessary interventions we need to get there in the present.

There we learned not only how to identify the weak signals that signify future trends here in the present, but also how to analyse potential future scenarios and develop the prototypes that we would need to be able to test those futures as alternative presents.

I documented everything I learned in a separate website with longform articles and videos, There’s too much to include here but please take a look, you might find something interesting in there!
